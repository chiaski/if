let me save you by knowing all of you
while we were sitting between the gunfire and the simulacrum
so you believe him to be interred with a scapular
while we dig with him at the grave. i want you there also
teeth scuffed, dirt mound, your appendix distorted
so barren of the things you once engorged.
maybe i will be so sorry. maybe then you will never
be alone. i'll bury you before the stories,
speak before you once more and abandon you
for a year. we take over the act of preservation
for you: mincing you words, invoking you in vain,
all this for you.
